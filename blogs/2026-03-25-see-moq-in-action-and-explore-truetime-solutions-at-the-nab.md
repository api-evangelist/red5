<h3 class="wp-block-heading" id="pod-1-openmoq-webrtc-hls-ll-hls-and-moq-latency-comparison-demo">Pod #1 OpenMOQ: WebRTC, HLS, LL-HLS, and MOQ Latency Comparison Demo</h3>



<p>This demo features a live camera feed from the show floor streamed back to the booth using multiple protocols simultaneously. You will see <a href="https://www.red5.net/media-over-quic-moq/">MOQ</a>, <a href="https://www.red5.net/webrtc/">WebRTC</a>, LL-HLS, and <a href="https://www.red5.net/hls-server/">HLS</a> side by side, making it easy to compare latency and performance in real time.</p>



<p>MOQ delivers near-instant, millisecond-level latency while maintaining scalability. Compared to traditional HTTP-based streaming and even WebRTC, it offers a simpler and more efficient approach to real-time delivery. At the same time, Red5 supports <a href="https://www.red5.net/video-streaming-technology/">all major protocols</a>, allowing you to choose what works best for your use case rather than forcing a single approach.</p>


<div class="wp-block-image">

</div>


<p class="has-text-align-center"><em>Red5 live streaming infrastructure supports WebRTC, RTMP, ERTMP, RSTP, HLS, SRT, WHIP and WHEP, Zixi video streaming protocols.</em></p>



<p>We will also highlight Red5’s <a href="https://www.red5.net/whitepapers/red5-server-side-ad-insertion/">server-side ad insertion capabilities</a>, developed in partnership with <a href="https://showfer.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">Showfer Media</a>. This enables real-time, personalized ad experiences across live streams and is already production-ready for WebRTC and HLS, with ongoing work to bring these capabilities to MOQ.</p>



<p>As a core <a href="https://www.red5.net/blog/red5-joined-openmoq/">contributor to the OpenMOQ ecosystem</a>, Red5 is working alongside companies like Cisco, Akamai, Bitmovin, and others to shape the future of live streaming through open standards.</p>

<p>Beyond the live demos, we are introducing how Red5 Cloud will deliver MOQ at scale through our partnership with <a href="https://www.red5.net/partners/cachefly/">CacheFly</a>. In this workflow, live streams are ingested into Red5, processed through our video packaging layer, and delivered via CacheFly using either MOQ or HTTP-based protocols like HLS and LL-HLS. This gives customers flexibility. You can deliver ultra-low latency streams with MOQ where real-time performance matters most, while still supporting traditional formats for broader device compatibility. It is not about replacing one protocol with another. It is about choosing what works best for your use case.
